Portfolio Management Services (PMS)

PMS is a professionally managed investment solution where a dedicated portfolio is run for the client, typically with higher customization, concentrated exposure, and active decision-making than traditional mutual funds. This page can be hosted as-is on GitHub Pages and updated anytime by replacing fund links or descriptions.

How PMS Works

In PMS, the client’s money is managed under a defined investment strategy by a professional fund manager or investment team. Unlike a mutual fund pool, the client usually gets a portfolio mapped to their own PMS account, giving greater portfolio visibility and a more direct strategy experience.

Typical PMS Journey

  • Client is onboarded after suitability and risk assessment.
  • The chosen strategy is selected based on return expectation, risk appetite, and holding period.
  • The PMS manager constructs and manages the portfolio actively.
  • Investments may be concentrated, theme-driven, value-oriented, growth-led, or income-focused.
  • Reporting is usually more transparent at the portfolio level than in traditional pooled products.

How PMS Differs from Mutual Funds

  • Higher degree of customization and active intervention.
  • Can run more concentrated, differentiated, and high-conviction portfolios.
  • Suitable for clients seeking alpha, strategy depth, or a sharper mandate.
  • Typically better suited for clients comfortable with market cycles and medium-to-long holding periods.

Benefits and Considerations

Benefits

  • Access to professional portfolio construction and active management.
  • Potential for differentiated alpha versus plain-vanilla diversified products.
  • Useful for sophisticated investors who want more than a standard mutual fund allocation.
  • Can complement an existing core portfolio with focused growth, value, contra, or income ideas.
  • Greater clarity on the strategy mandate and portfolio style.

Cons / Risks

  • Higher risk than broad diversified allocation if the strategy is concentrated.
  • Performance may be cyclical and can underperform for stretches.
  • Requires client maturity, patience, and ability to absorb interim volatility.
  • Not ideal for very short-term money or for clients needing guaranteed outcomes.
  • Fees, taxation, drawdowns, and liquidity expectations should be explained upfront.

Whom to Pitch PMS To

PMS should be positioned selectively, not universally.

Best Fit Client Profiles

  • Clients who already have a base mutual fund portfolio and want an alpha-seeking satellite allocation.
  • Investors comfortable with a 3–5+ year horizon.
  • Clients who understand market volatility and do not panic with temporary drawdowns.
  • HNI / affluent investors seeking strategy-led allocation rather than generic diversification.
  • Clients looking for differentiated exposure such as contra, compounders, small & mid-cap, or income enhancement.

Avoid / Use Caution

  • First-time equity investors without market experience.
  • Clients with short-term liquidity needs.
  • Clients seeking capital protection or assured return language.
  • Highly anxious investors who track prices daily and react emotionally.
  • Clients without clear suitability alignment.
Practical pitch: Position PMS as a satellite allocation for suitable clients who already have a stable core portfolio and want a professionally managed, higher-conviction strategy for long-term wealth creation or differentiated income generation.
